# X/Twitter Ads Blocking Analysis — Neobank Radar

*Date: 2025-04-25*

## Why X/Twitter Is Likely Blocking Your Ads

The site triggers multiple X advertising policy flags simultaneously.

### 1. Financial Products & Services Policy (most likely trigger)

X requires pre-authorization for advertisers promoting financial products. The site is a comparison platform for financial products — even though it's not selling a specific card, X's automated systems classify it the same way.

Key policy: advertisers promoting financial services must be certified through X's Financial Products certification process. This applies to comparison sites too.

### 2. Cryptocurrency Policy (high-probability trigger)

X has a dedicated Cryptocurrency Products and Services policy. The site prominently features:

- "crypto debit card", "crypto credit card", "DeFi card", "Web3 card"
- APY/APR claims: "up to 14% interest", "10% APY on crypto", "7% APY"
- "Airdrop farming" as a feature filter
- Stablecoin and DeFi references throughout

X requires crypto advertisers to be pre-approved and restricts ads in many jurisdictions. The automated review likely flags these terms immediately.

### 3. Specific Content Red Flags

| Content on the site                          | Why X flags it                                       |
| -------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| "No KYC" as a filterable feature             | Implies facilitating unregulated financial activity   |
| "Privacy-Friendly: Reduced KYC requirements" | Same — suggests circumventing compliance              |
| APY/APR claims (4%-20% APY)                  | Looks like investment return promises                 |
| "Earn up to 14% interest per year"           | Financial return claims require disclosures           |
| "Airdrop Farming" filter + descriptions      | Speculative crypto activity promotion                 |
| "Self-Custody: you hold your own keys"       | Unregulated wallet promotion in some jurisdictions    |

### 4. The Disclaimer Is Good But Insufficient for X

The footer disclaimer ("does not constitute financial advice...") is the right approach for a website, but X's ad review looks at the landing page content holistically, not just the disclaimer.

---

## What You Can Do

### Option A: Get X Ad Certification (recommended)

1. Apply for Financial Products certification on X Ads: go to your X Ads account > Brand Safety > Certifications > Financial Products & Services
2. Apply for Cryptocurrency certification (separate from financial products)
3. You'll need to provide: business registration, website URL, description of services, and potentially compliance documentation
4. Once certified, your ads will be allowed in approved markets

### Option B: Create a "clean" landing page for ads

If certification takes too long, create an alternate landing page that:

- Positions as educational content, not product comparison ("Learn about digital banking" instead of "Compare crypto cards")
- Removes financial trigger words from the page X sees: no APY/APR percentages, no "earn", "interest", "yield", "returns", no "No KYC" or "privacy-friendly" framing, no "airdrop farming"
- Emphasizes the informational nature: "Research platform" or "Educational guide"
- Links to the full comparison site from there (X reviews the landing page, not deep links)

### Option C: Adjust your ad copy (less likely to fix it alone)

If the website is the trigger (most likely), copy changes alone won't help. But to rule out copy as the issue:

**Avoid these terms in ad copy:**

- "crypto", "cryptocurrency", "DeFi", "Web3"
- "cashback", "earn", "rewards", "APY"
- "No KYC", "self-custody"
- Any specific financial product names

**Safer framing:**

- "Compare digital banking products" instead of "Compare crypto cards"
- "Research financial tools" instead of "Find the best neobank card"

### Recommendation

Go with Option A (certification). The site is legitimate — it has a proper disclaimer, doesn't sell any financial product directly, and is an informational comparison platform. The certification process exists exactly for legitimate businesses like this. The "No KYC" and "Airdrop Farming" features are factual product attributes, not promotion of illegal activity — but X's automated systems can't make that distinction without the certification.

In parallel, consider Option B as a short-term workaround: a stripped-down /landing page with educational framing used exclusively as the ad destination URL.
